In diabetes care and health screening, **HbA1c** is one of the most commonly cited numbers. It reflects average blood sugar over the past 2–3 months and is considered the gold standard for assessing long-term glucose control.

## What is HbA1c?

HbA1c forms when glucose binds to hemoglobin in red blood cells. Because <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK431089/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">red blood cells live about 120 days</a>, HbA1c reflects average blood glucose over the previous 8–12 weeks, per the <a href="https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/diagnostic-tests/a1c-test" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NIDDK</a>. It is not affected by short-term fluctuations from a single meal or workout, so it gives a broader picture than a one-time reading.

## Why is the A1C test important?

The A1C test is used to:

- Diagnose diabetes and prediabetes
- Assess average glucose control over 2–3 months
- Guide treatment decisions
- Estimate risk of diabetes complications

## What are target A1C levels?

A1C targets are individualized per the <a href="https://care.diabetesjournals.org/content/47/Supplement_1/S153" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ADA glycemic control standards</a>. General categories are:

- Normal: below 5.7%
- Prediabetes: 5.7%–6.4%
- Diabetes: 6.5% or higher
- Common goal for many adults with diabetes: below 7%, per <a href="https://care.diabetesjournals.org/content/47/Supplement_1/S153" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ADA</a>

## A1C vs daily glucose readings

A1C is an average, so it does not show glucose variability. Two people with the same A1C can have very different daily swings. That is why daily self-monitoring of fasting and post-meal glucose remains important.

## How to improve HbA1c

The key to a better A1C is long-term glucose stability. Strategies include dietary changes, regular exercise, weight management, prescribed medications, quitting smoking, limiting alcohol, good sleep, and stress management.

## Conclusion

HbA1c is an essential marker of long-term control, but it does not replace daily monitoring. Use both A1C and routine glucose checks to get a complete view of your metabolic health.
